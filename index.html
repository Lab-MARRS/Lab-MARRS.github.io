Welcome to MARRS

Monash Research Center of Automated and Resilient Road Systems (MARRS), which is established in 2018 and expanded in 2024, is lead by A/Prof. Nan Zheng. Our research lab is dedicated to advancing the frontiers of science and technology in transportation through innovative research and collaboration. We focus on cutting-edge technologies and interdisciplinary approaches to solve complex problems that impact society, including urban transport, freight transport and robotic vehicles.

We are committed to excellence in research, education, and service. Our lab provides a supportive and collaborative environment where creativity and innovation thrive. We welcome collaborations with industry partners, other academic institutions, and researchers from around the world.

Lab Director

Dr. Nan.Zheng

Associate Professor & Lab Director

Dr. Nan Zheng is an associate professor and the founding director of our research lab. received his BSc, MSc. and PhD degrees, in Transportation Engineering, from the Southeast University, Delft University of Technology, École polytechnique fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL), in 2007, 2009, and 2014, respectively.

Dr. Nan Zheng was previously appointed as a Post-doc by EPFL and the Federal Institute of Technology Zurich (ETHZ), then as an Associate Professor at the School of Transportation at Beihang University. Since 2018, A/Prof. Nan Zheng has been employed at the Civil and Environmental Engineering Department at Monash University.

Dr. Nan Zheng’s researches focus on on macro traffic flow theory, regional traffic control and optimization, autonomous driving system training, safety assessment, and transportation service application.

Dr. Nan Zheng is Associate Editor of IEEE Transaction on Intelligent Vehicles (Q1), Transportmetrica B: Transport Dynamics (Q1), Journal of Intelligent and Connected Vehicles (new journal); an editorial board member of Transportation Research Part C (Q1): Emerging Technologies.

Research Areas

Robotics & Autonomous Systems

We research intelligent robotics systems that can operate autonomously in complex environments. Our work includes robot perception, motion planning, and human-robot interaction. We develop algorithms for mobile robots, manipulation systems, and multi-robot coordination with applications in manufacturing, healthcare, and service industries.

Robot Perception Path Planning Human-Robot Interaction

Machine Learning & Artificial Intelligence

Our research in machine learning focuses on developing novel algorithms and methodologies for solving complex real-world problems. We work on deep learning, reinforcement learning, and transfer learning with applications in computer vision, natural language processing, and autonomous systems. Our goal is to create intelligent systems that can learn, adapt, and make decisions in dynamic environments.

Deep Learning Neural Networks Computer Vision

Data Science & Analytics

We develop advanced data mining and analytics techniques to extract meaningful insights from large-scale datasets. Our research encompasses statistical modeling, predictive analytics, and visualization methods. We collaborate with various domains including transport, freight, and robot to solve data-driven challenges and support evidence-based decision making.

Big Data Statistical Modeling Visualization
